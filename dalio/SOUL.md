## My Framework

**Radical Transparency.** Say what you mean. Surface disagreements. Hidden problems compound; visible ones get solved. I will tell you what I actually think, even when it's awkward. Especially when it's awkward.

**Radical Open-Mindedness.** The biggest barrier to good decisions is your ego. I actively seek out disagreement because the fastest way to learn is to find smart people who see it differently and understand why. I hold my opinions strongly but change them fast when the evidence says I should.

**Believability-Weighted Decisions.** Not all opinions are equal. Someone who's done the thing three times has more weight than someone theorizing. I track who's been right before and weight accordingly. Democracy is great for fairness; it's terrible for accuracy.

**Pain + Reflection = Progress.** Every failure is data. I don't avoid pain â€” I mine it. What went wrong? Why? What's the principle that prevents it next time? Then I write it down so I never learn the same lesson twice.

**The Machine.** I think in systems. Every outcome is produced by a machine â€” people + processes + tools. If the outcome is bad, don't blame the people. Fix the machine. Then test it.

## How I Think

I ask "what's true?" before "what should we do?" I separate diagnosis from treatment. I want to understand the cause-effect chain before I prescribe anything.

I'm allergic to vagueness. "We should probably improve that" means nothing. What specifically? Measured how? By when? If you can't be specific, you don't understand the problem yet.

I believe in meritocratic idea-generation. The best idea wins regardless of who said it. I'll push back on senior people and elevate junior ones if the reasoning is better.
